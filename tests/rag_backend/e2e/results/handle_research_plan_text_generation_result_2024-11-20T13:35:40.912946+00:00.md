#### Aim 1: Discovery campaign of cytokine candidates with optimal synergistic effects with anti-TREM2 antibody
The research aim focuses on identifying cytokine candidates that exhibit optimal synergistic effects when combined with the anti-TREM2 monoclonal antibody (mAb) E3C7. The working hypothesis posits that specific cytokines, when paired with E3C7, can enhance immune cell activation and tumor cell cytotoxicity, thereby improving therapeutic outcomes in cancer treatment. The general goals include screening approximately 30 cytokines to determine their efficacy in combination with E3C7, selecting the most effective cytokines, and understanding the molecular mechanisms underlying their synergistic effects.

 The methodology involves a multi-step approach, beginning with in vitro assays to evaluate the effects of cytokine and E3C7 combinations on myeloid, natural killer (NK), and T cell activity. This includes measuring cytokine-induced changes in cell proliferation, activation, and cytokine secretion. Killing assays will be conducted to assess the cytotoxic potential of these combinations against human-derived cancer cells. The most promising cytokines will be selected based on their performance in these assays.

 Subsequently, single-cell analysis will be employed using both in vitro and in vivo functional screening systems. The in vitro system will allow for detailed examination of cytokine effects at the single-cell level, providing insights into cellular heterogeneity and response variability. The in vivo system will utilize Pro-Code tumor cell lines to enable inducible over-expression of cytokines and their combinations within the tumor microenvironment (TME), facilitating spatial delineation of cytokine-driven effects. This comprehensive analysis aims to elucidate the downstream molecular pathways activated by the cytokine-E3C7 combinations.

 Expected results include the identification of cytokine candidates that significantly enhance the efficacy of E3C7, characterized by increased immune cell activation and tumor cell killing. The study anticipates uncovering specific cytokine signatures that correlate with improved therapeutic outcomes, thereby informing the development of novel cytokine-mAb fusion proteins for cancer immunotherapy. The integration of machine learning analytics will further refine the selection process, ensuring the identification of the most effective cytokine combinations. This research is expected to contribute to the advancement of targeted cancer therapies by leveraging the synergistic potential of cytokines and anti-TREM2 antibodies.

 ##### Research Tasks

###### Task 1.1: Measure cytokine and anti-TREM2 effects on myeloid, NK and T cell activity
The objective of this research task (1.1) is to evaluate the effects of cytokines in conjunction with the E3C7 monoclonal antibody (mAb) on the activity of myeloid, natural killer (NK), and T cells. This will be achieved through a series of in-vitro assays involving co-cultured human monocyte-derived macrophages (huMDMs), NK cells, and T cells. The experimental design includes control assays that will be conducted without the anti-TREM2 mAb and without the addition of cytokines to establish baseline immune cell activity. The primary endpoints will be the assessment of NK and T cell activity, which will be quantified by measuring proliferative potential through flow cytometry and cytokine secretion via enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) and quantitative polymerase chain reaction (qPCR).

 Data collection will involve systematic sampling of immune cell responses across multiple experimental conditions, ensuring replicates are included to enhance statistical power. Flow cytometry will provide quantitative data on cell proliferation, while ELISA and qPCR will yield qualitative and quantitative insights into cytokine production. The integration of these methodologies will facilitate a comprehensive analysis of the immune response dynamics under varying conditions of cytokine and mAb exposure.

 Results will be analyzed using appropriate statistical methods to determine the significance of differences observed between experimental groups. Flow cytometry data will be processed using specialized software to quantify cell populations and their activation states, while ELISA and qPCR results will be subjected to comparative analysis to elucidate the impact of the treatments on cytokine secretion profiles. The interpretation framework will focus on correlating the observed immune cell activity with the presence of cytokines and the anti-TREM2 mAb, aiming to identify synergistic effects that could inform future therapeutic strategies targeting immune modulation in disease contexts. This task is designed to be completed within a defined timeframe, ensuring that results can be integrated into subsequent phases of the broader research initiative aimed at optimizing anti-TREM2 therapies for enhanced immune response.

###### Task 1.2: Killing assays.
 The objective of this research task (1.2) is to evaluate the synergistic effects of various cytokines in conjunction with the E3C7 monoclonal antibody (mAb) on the activation of cytotoxic T and natural killer (NK) cells against tumor cells. The experimental design will incorporate multiple treatment groups, where assays will be treated with different combinations of cytokines and E3C7 mAb, alongside control groups receiving only the individual components (cytokines or E3C7 mAb). The methodology will involve pre-staining tumor cells to facilitate quantification of cell death via fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS).

 Data collection will focus on measuring NK and T cell activity, specifically assessing cytotoxicity levels through the quantification of tumor cell death. This will be accomplished by analyzing FACS data to determine the percentage of dead tumor cells in each treatment group compared to controls. The experimental setup will ensure that all assays are conducted under identical conditions to minimize variability and enhance reproducibility.

 Results will be analyzed using statistical methods appropriate for comparing multiple treatment groups, such as ANOVA, followed by post-hoc tests to identify significant differences between specific combinations of cytokines and E3C7 mAb. Interpretation of the results will focus on identifying which cytokine combinations yield the highest levels of cytotoxic activity, thereby elucidating potential mechanisms of action and informing future therapeutic strategies. The task is designed to be completed within a defined timeframe, ensuring that all assays are conducted and analyzed within the project’s overall timeline, thereby contributing to the broader objectives of the research program aimed at developing effective immunotherapeutic strategies against cancer.

#### Aim 2: Design, development and production of novel mAb-cytokine fusion molecules
The research aim focuses on the design, development, and production of novel monoclonal antibody (mAb)-cytokine fusion molecules, with a specific emphasis on validating their pro-inflammatory and antineoplastic activities. The working hypothesis posits that these fusion molecules can effectively modulate immune responses to enhance anti-tumor activity while minimizing systemic toxicity. The general goals include the creation of innovative mAb-cytokine constructs, their production at scale, and comprehensive validation of their biological activities.

 The methodology encompasses several key tasks: designing fusion proteins with optimal cleavage sites, producing these proteins, and conducting rigorous in silico and in vitro validations. The design phase involves computational modeling to predict the most effective fusion configurations, ensuring that the cytokine component retains its functional integrity while being conjugated to the mAb. Production will utilize recombinant DNA technology to express these fusion proteins in suitable host systems, followed by purification and characterization to confirm structural and functional fidelity.

 Validation of the fusion proteins will be conducted through a series of assays. In silico analyses will predict binding affinities and potential off-target effects, while in vitro assays will assess the biological activity of the fusion proteins. These assays will include evaluating the effects of the molecules on myeloid, natural killer (NK), and T cell activity, as well as their efficacy in NK and T cell-mediated cytotoxicity assays. The expected results are that the mAb-cytokine fusion molecules will demonstrate enhanced activation of immune effector cells and increased tumor cell killing compared to non-fusion counterparts.

 The research leverages advanced multi-omics pipelines and machine learning analytics to identify optimal cytokine candidates for fusion, as outlined in the EIC Transition objectives (EIC Transition 2022-2024, HORIZON-EIC-2022-TRANSITION-01, MiTE). This approach aims to achieve a molecular understanding of the downstream effects of each treatment combination, facilitating the selection of the most effective fusion constructs. Ultimately, the successful development and validation of these fusion molecules could lead to novel therapeutic strategies for cancer treatment, with the potential for clinical translation and commercialization.

 The expected results include the identification of the most effective MiTE molecules with optimal dosing regimens and treatment courses, demonstrating significant anti-tumor activity and TME reprogramming capabilities.
