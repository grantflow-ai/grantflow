# Regulation of organic reactions by controlled microenvironments using designer condensates

## Abstract

This Proof of Concept project addresses the environmental challenges of traditional drug synthesis by employing biomolecular condensates as tunable, organic solvent-free reaction systems. The project aims to revolutionize green drug synthesis, offering a sustainable alternative to conventional methods. Our approach leverages liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) to create biocompatible microenvironments that enhance reaction efficiency. The objectives include developing and optimizing conditions for antibody-drug conjugate click reactions, characterizing reactant and product encapsulation within condensates, and optimizing reaction kinetics. We will employ amphipathic peptide sequences to control LLPS behavior and condensate properties, focusing on aromatic residue content and charge distribution patterns. Expected outcomes include establishing parameters that maximize ADC formation efficiency while maintaining antibody stability, quantifying encapsulation efficiency using fluorescence correlation spectroscopy and multi-angle dynamic light scattering, and correlating peptide sequence features with encapsulation efficiency. The project's impact lies in its potential to enable sustainable drug synthesis in an organic solvent-free aqueous environment, aligning with green chemistry principles and reducing environmental impact. The technology's dynamic and tunable nature, combined with its scalability and biocompatibility, positions it for significant commercial viability in the pharmaceutical industry. This ERC Proof of Concept grant will facilitate the transition of this innovative research towards industrial implementation and commercialization.
