# Regulation of organic reactions by controlled microenvironments using designer condensates

## Abstract

This Proof of Concept project addresses the critical need for sustainable drug synthesis by employing biomolecular condensates as tunable, organic solvent-free reaction systems. The project aims to revolutionize green drug synthesis, offering an environmentally friendly alternative to traditional methods that rely on harmful organic solvents. The central objective is to leverage liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) to create biocompatible microenvironments that enhance reaction efficiency and control reactant recruitment and catalytic activity. The methodology involves designing peptide sequences that drive LLPS, forming condensates with tailored architectures and material properties. These condensates will serve as microreactors, enabling precise spatial regulation of hydrophobic organic molecules and their chemical transformations. Expected outcomes include the development of a versatile platform for a variety of chemical and enzymatic reactions, enhanced reaction rates and conversion, and a significant reduction in waste generation. The project's impact lies in its potential to transform pharmaceutical manufacturing by promoting sustainable practices and reducing environmental impact. By eliminating the reliance on organic solvents and leveraging biomolecular systems, this approach offers a sustainable and scalable alternative to traditional methods. The technology's biocompatibility allows potential integration into biomedical and pharmaceutical applications, enhancing its commercial viability and market potential within the green chemistry sector.
