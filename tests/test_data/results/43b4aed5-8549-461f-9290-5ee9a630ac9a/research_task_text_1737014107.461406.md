###### Task 1.1: Temporal understanding of immune activity in BM TME

This research task focuses on elucidating the temporal dynamics of immune cell activity within the Bone Marrow Tumor Microenvironment (BM TME). The primary objective is to employ Zman-seq, a technology that introduces temporal information to single-cell RNA sequencing data, to track immune cell state transitions over time in the BM TME. The project adapts the Zman-seq methodology, previously validated in glioma models, to the BM microenvironment.   The experimental design involves in vivo labeling of circulating immune cells with a sequence of fluorophores and tracking these labeled cells as they infiltrate the BM TME. Specifically, fluorescent anti-CD45 antibodies are injected intravenously at various time points before tissue harvest, enabling the determination of tumor exposure time for each immune cell. The collected cells are then profiled using fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS) and index sorting to capture fluorescent timestamps and their corresponding transcriptomic profiles via scRNA-seq. This approach allows for the construction of temporal maps of the BM TME. Analysis will involve generating continuous tumor exposure time (cTET) values for each cell, which enables the temporal ordering of cells and the identification of gene expression changes associated with different stages of immune cell infiltration. The analysis will focus on deciphering temporal gene modules, TF circuits, and receptor-ligand interactions that drive immune responses and evasion mechanisms within the BM TME. This approach is expected to resolve dynamic cell-cell interactions and their influence on cell states, revealing causal relationships during cellular transitions. Statistical methods will be used to identify significant gene expression changes across the temporal axis, along with trajectory inference to reconstruct differentiation pathways. This research task does not involve clinical trials. The application of Zman-seq to the BM TME will provide insights into the dynamics of immune dysfunction and may help to develop novel immunotherapeutic strategies by identifying critical checkpoints of immune cell differentiation. This task builds upon previous research that adapted Zman-seq from glioma to the BM microenvironment. The crucial technical terms include Zman-seq, scRNA-seq, FACS, tumor microenvironment (TME), tumor exposure time, and cell state transitions.   While the general experimental approach is well established from previous work, a specific experimental protocol adapted for the BM is not fully detailed in the provided information. Potential challenges include technical difficulties in adapting the Zman-seq protocol to the BM and in distinguishing immune cell populations in the BM TME. The use of appropriate antibody concentrations and appropriate controls needs to be carefully assessed. This research has the potential to significantly enhance our understanding of immune responses in the BM TME, paving the way for novel therapeutic interventions.
