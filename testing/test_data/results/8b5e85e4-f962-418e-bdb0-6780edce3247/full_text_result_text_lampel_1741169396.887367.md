# Regulation of organic reactions by controlled microenvironments using designer condensates

## Abstract

This Proof of Concept project addresses the critical need for sustainable drug synthesis by employing biomolecular condensates as tunable, organic solvent-free reaction systems. The project aims to revolutionize green drug synthesis, offering an environmentally friendly alternative to traditional methods that rely on harmful organic solvents. The central objective is to leverage liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) to create biocompatible microenvironments that enhance reaction efficiency and control reactant recruitment and catalytic activity. The methodology involves designing peptide sequences that drive LLPS, forming condensates with tailored architectures and material properties. These condensates will serve as microreactors, enabling precise spatial regulation of hydrophobic organic molecules and their chemical transformations. Expected outcomes include the development of a versatile platform for a variety of chemical and enzymatic reactions, enhanced reaction rates and conversion, and a significant reduction in waste generation. The project's impact lies in its potential to transform pharmaceutical manufacturing by promoting sustainable practices and reducing environmental impact. The technology's scalability and tunability offer significant commercial viability, with potential applications in various industries seeking greener and more efficient chemical processes. This ERC Proof of Concept project will validate the technology's potential for commercialization, paving the way for its adoption in the pharmaceutical and chemical sectors.

### Objective 1: Develop and optimize conditions for antibody-drug conjugate click reactions as model systems

Objective 1 aims to develop click chemistry conditions within LLPS-based microreactors for ADC synthesis. Peptide sequences with alternating arginine and valine residues will promote LLPS and partition reactants into condensates. CuAAC reaction kinetics and efficiency within condensates will be compared to traditional organic solvents using mass spectrometry. Parameters including peptide sequence, reactant concentration (0.1-10 mM), and temperature (20-40 °C) will be optimized to maximize yields. Success criteria include >80% conversion to desired ADC product within condensates.

This objective will establish how condensate architecture influences molecular retention, informing microreactor design for sustainable drug synthesis and reaction optimization in Objective 3, facilitating environmentally friendly alternatives to traditional solvent-based methods.

### Objective 3: Characterization of reactions in condensates

Objective 3 focuses on characterizing reaction kinetics and efficiency within LLPS condensates to advance green chemistry practices through reduced organic solvent usage. Reaction rates and conversions will be quantified using mass spectrometry to monitor ADC product formation and NMR spectroscopy to assess reaction kinetics. The influence of condensate composition on reaction dynamics will be examined by varying arginine/valine ratios, building upon Objective 2's encapsulation data. Product localization between dense and dilute phases will be determined via confocal microscopy. This objective will establish quantitative understanding of reaction dynamics within condensates, informing sustainable reaction system development and scale-up efforts in Objective 4.
