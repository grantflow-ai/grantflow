Call for Proposals: 2025 NIH Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I

FUNDING OPPORTUNITY ANNOUNCEMENT

1. Overview
The National Institutes of Health (NIH) invites Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Phase I proposals that demonstrate the technical merit and commercial potential of innovative biomedical technologies. Awards provide funding to establish proof of concept and feasibility for commercialization.

2. Funding Details
Phase I Award Amount: Up to $500,000 (direct costs)
Project Period: 12 months maximum
Indirect Costs: Limited to 25% of direct costs
Cost-sharing: Not required but encouraged

3. Eligibility Criteria
To be eligible, applicants must:
• Be a for-profit small business concern
• Have fewer than 500 employees
• Be majority-owned by U.S. citizens or permanent residents
• Perform at least 67% of the research work
• Have principal place of business in the United States

4. Application Components
Complete applications must include:
• Research Strategy (6 pages maximum)
  - Significance and Innovation (1 page)
  - Approach (4 pages)
  - Commercialization Plan (1 page)
• Budget and Budget Justification (no page limit)
• Biographical Sketches (5 pages per person maximum)
• Research Plan Attachments
• Letters of Support from potential customers or partners

5. Research Strategy Requirements
The research strategy must clearly describe:
• Specific aims and objectives
• Background and rationale
• Preliminary data (if available)
• Experimental design and methods
• Expected outcomes and success criteria
• Potential obstacles and alternative approaches

6. Commercialization Plan
Applicants must provide a detailed plan addressing:
• Market analysis and target customers
• Competitive landscape assessment
• Intellectual property strategy
• Business model and revenue projections
• Regulatory pathway (if applicable)
• Timeline for Phase II application and product development

7. Review Criteria
Applications will be evaluated on:
1. Significance (25%) - Importance of problem being addressed
2. Innovation (25%) - Novelty of approach and technology
3. Approach (25%) - Technical merit and feasibility
4. Investigator(s) (15%) - Qualifications and track record
5. Commercialization Potential (10%) - Market opportunity and business plan

8. Submission Deadlines
Standard Receipt Dates:
• April 5, 2025
• August 5, 2025  
• December 5, 2025

Applications must be submitted by 5:00 PM local time of applicant organization.

9. Award Information
Phase I awards are typically made within 6-9 months of submission deadline. Successful Phase I awardees will be eligible to apply for Phase II funding (up to $3,000,000 over 2-3 years).

Phase II applications must be submitted within 2 years of Phase I award date.

10. Application Submission
All applications must be submitted electronically through Grants.gov. Paper applications will not be accepted. Applicants should allow adequate time for Grants.gov registration process, which can take several weeks.
