Department of Energy Office of Science
Funding Opportunity Announcement: Early Career Research Program 2025

DE-FOA-0003121

1. Program Description
The DOE Office of Science Early Career Research Program supports the development of individual research programs of outstanding scientists early in their careers and stimulates research careers in the disciplines supported by the DOE Office of Science.

2. Award Information
Number of Awards: Approximately 65-75 awards
Total Program Funding: $27,000,000
Individual Award Amount: $150,000 per year for 5 years (total $750,000)
Project Period: 60 months
Cost Share: Not required

3. Eligibility Requirements

Principal Investigator Qualifications:
• Must be employed full-time at a U.S. academic institution or DOE national laboratory
• Must have received Ph.D. within the 10 years prior to the application deadline
• Must not have received the DOE Early Career Award previously
• Career interruptions will be taken into consideration

Eligible Institutions:
• Accredited U.S. universities and colleges
• DOE national laboratories
• Non-profit research institutions

4. Research Areas
Applications are accepted in the following Office of Science program areas:
• Advanced Scientific Computing Research
• Basic Energy Sciences
• Biological and Environmental Research
• Fusion Energy Sciences
• High Energy Physics
• Nuclear Physics

Proposals must clearly align with one of these research areas and demonstrate relevance to DOE mission.

5. Application Requirements

Proposal Page Limits:
• Project Narrative: 10 pages maximum
• Project Summary: 1 page maximum
• Bibliography and References Cited: 4 pages maximum
• Biographical Sketch: 2 pages maximum
• Current and Pending Support: No page limit
• Budget Justification: 3 pages maximum

Required Elements of Project Narrative:
1. Background and Significance (2-3 pages)
2. Preliminary Results (if applicable, 1-2 pages)
3. Proposed Research and Methods (4-5 pages)
4. Timeline and Milestones (1 page)
5. Broader Impacts (1 page)

6. Formatting Requirements
• Font size: 11 point or larger
• Margins: 1 inch on all sides
• Line spacing: Single space
• Page numbering: Required
• File format: PDF only

Figures, tables, and equations are included in the page count. References may use smaller font (minimum 10 point).

7. Evaluation and Selection Criteria

Technical Merit (60%):
• Scientific and/or technical merit of proposed research
• Innovativeness and originality of research approach
• Appropriateness of proposed method or approach
• Qualifications of the principal investigator
• Availability of personnel, facilities, equipment, and other resources

Relevance to DOE Mission (40%):
• Relevance to the DOE Office of Science research areas
• Potential for advancing DOE mission objectives
• Alignment with program priorities and strategic goals

8. Budget Guidelines
Allowable Costs:
• Personnel (PI salary support up to 2 summer months or equivalent)
• Graduate student support and postdoctoral researcher support
• Travel for research purposes (not to exceed $5,000 per year)
• Equipment necessary for conduct of proposed research
• Supplies and materials
• Computer time

Non-allowable Costs:
• Construction or major renovation of facilities
• General purpose equipment
• Administrative costs exceeding 25% of direct costs

9. Submission Process
Applications must be submitted through:
• For universities and non-profit institutions: Grants.gov
• For DOE national laboratories: DOE Field Work Proposal process

Submission Deadline: 5:00 PM ET on February 1, 2025

10. Timeline
• Application deadline: February 1, 2025
• Peer review period: March - May 2025
• Selection decisions: June 2025
• Award notifications: July 2025
• Project start date: September 1, 2025

11. Additional Requirements

Data Management Plan:
A Data Management Plan of no more than 2 pages must describe how research data will be shared and preserved or explain why data sharing and/or preservation are not possible or scientifically appropriate.

Human Subjects and Animal Welfare:
Research involving human subjects or live vertebrate animals requires appropriate approvals and documentation.

Environmental Safety and Health:
All activities must comply with applicable ES&H requirements and receive necessary approvals before work begins.

12. Post-Award Requirements
• Annual progress reports due 60 days after each budget period
• Final technical report due 90 days after project completion
• Acknowledgment of DOE support in all publications
• Data sharing as specified in Data Management Plan
• Participation in Early Career Research Program meeting (travel funded)
