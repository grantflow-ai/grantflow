Request for Proposals: 2025 NSF Collaborative Research Program

1. Program Summary
The National Science Foundation (NSF) invites proposals for collaborative research projects that address fundamental questions in science and engineering. This program supports interdisciplinary teams working on transformative research with potential for broad impact. The maximum award amount is $1,200,000 over three years.

2. Eligibility Requirements
• Principal Investigator must be affiliated with an accredited U.S. institution
• Proposals must involve at least two institutions
• Early-career investigators (within 7 years of PhD) are particularly encouraged
• International collaborations are welcome but funding is limited to U.S. participants

3. Proposal Requirements
Applications must not exceed 15 pages for the project description and include:
• Specific Aims (2 pages maximum)
• Background and Significance (4 pages maximum)
• Preliminary Data (3 pages maximum)
• Research Plan and Methods (6 pages maximum)
• Timeline and Milestones
• Budget Justification
• Biographical sketches for all investigators
• Letters of collaboration from partner institutions

4. Budget Guidelines
• Maximum total award: $1,200,000 over 36 months
• Direct costs only - indirect costs are not allowed
• Equipment purchases over $5,000 require special justification
• Travel funds may not exceed 10% of total budget
• Student support is encouraged and should be detailed

5. Evaluation Criteria
Proposals will be evaluated based on:
• Intellectual Merit (40%)
• Broader Impacts (30%)
• Collaboration Plan (20%)
• Budget Appropriateness (10%)

Reviewers will assess scientific excellence, innovation potential, team qualifications, and feasibility of the proposed work.

6. Important Dates
Letter of Intent deadline: January 15, 2025
Full proposal deadline: March 15, 2025
Awards announced: September 1, 2025
Project start date: October 1, 2025

7. Submission Requirements
All proposals must be submitted through NSFPropSpace.gov using the standard NSF proposal format. Late submissions will not be accepted under any circumstances. Proposals should follow NSF formatting guidelines including 11-point font, single spacing, and 1-inch margins.
