# Regulation of organic reactions by controlled microenvironments using designer condensates

## Abstract

This Proof of Concept project addresses the environmental challenges of traditional drug synthesis by employing biomolecular condensates as tunable, organic solvent-free reaction systems. The project aims to revolutionize green drug synthesis, offering a sustainable and efficient alternative to conventional methods. Objectives include optimizing conditions for antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) click reactions within peptide-based condensates, characterizing reactant and product encapsulation, analyzing reaction kinetics, and establishing system sustainability and scalability. The methodology leverages liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) to create biocompatible microenvironments that enhance reaction efficiency. Expected outcomes include validated solvent-free ADC synthesis protocols with >90% yields, >100-fold reactant concentration enhancement, and scalable processes for various chemical and enzymatic reactions. The project's impact lies in enabling drug synthesis in an organic solvent-free environment, aligning with green chemistry principles and reducing environmental impact. The technology's dynamic and tunable nature, combined with its biocompatibility, positions it for integration into biomedical and pharmaceutical applications. Successful commercialization will transform pharmaceutical manufacturing through scalable, biocompatible microenvironments, offering a sustainable and economically viable alternative to traditional methods. This ERC Proof of Concept grant will facilitate the path to industrialization and commercialization of this innovative technology.
