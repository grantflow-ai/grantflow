# Regulation of organic reactions by controlled microenvironments using designer condensates

## Abstract

This Proof of Concept project addresses the environmental challenges of traditional drug and macromolecule-drug conjugate synthesis by employing biomolecular condensates as tunable, organic solvent-free reaction systems. The project aims to revolutionize green drug synthesis, offering a sustainable alternative to conventional methods. Our central hypothesis posits that the chemical composition of peptide building blocks dictates the spatial regulation, reaction rate, and conversion within condensates, thereby controlling condensate architecture and material properties. The objectives include developing and optimizing conditions for antibody-drug conjugate click reactions, characterizing reactant and product encapsulation, and optimizing reaction kinetics within condensates. We will leverage liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) to design biocompatible microenvironments that enhance reaction efficiency. Expected outcomes include the elimination of organic solvents, enhanced reaction efficiency, and biocompatible systems suitable for pharmaceutical applications. The potential impact lies in enabling sustainable drug synthesis in an organic solvent-free aqueous environment, aligning with green chemistry principles. The technology's advantages include environmental friendliness, dynamic tunability, scalability, enhanced reactivity, and biocompatibility. ## Section 1a: The idea – Breakthrough Innovation potential

### 1a.i. Brief description of the idea to be taken to proof of concept

The environmental impact of traditional drug and macromolecule-drug conjugate (ADC) synthesis, heavily reliant on organic solvents, poses significant sustainability challenges. This proof-of-concept (PoC) project addresses this problem by employing biomolecular condensates as dynamic, tunable, organic solvent-free reaction systems for green drug synthesis. The proposed solution leverages liquid-liquid phase separation (LLPS) to create sustainable, biocompatible microenvironments that enhance reaction efficiency. This is achieved through precise control over reactant recruitment and catalytic activity within the condensates, eliminating the need for organic solvents and reducing waste generation. The value proposition lies in providing a sustainable and efficient alternative to traditional organic solvent-based methods, aligning with green chemistry principles.

The core technology builds upon the principles of micellar catalysis, enabling organic reactions in solvent-free aqueous media. The novelty lies in the enhanced versatility and tunability achieved through condensates, offering precise control over chemical composition and reaction parameters. Potential applications include the green synthesis of ADCs, reducing the environmental footprint of pharmaceutical manufacturing and enhancing the biocompatibility of drug products. The expected outcome is a validated platform for sustainable drug synthesis, demonstrating the feasibility and efficiency of biomolecular condensates as reaction microenvironments.

This PoC project originates from an ERC-funded project focused on developing peptide-based bioinspired materials with tunable supramolecular order. The scientific basis is the hypothesis that the chemical composition of the peptide sequence determines the spatial regulation of hydrophobic organic molecules and their chemical transformation within condensates.
